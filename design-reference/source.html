§ 02 — Mechanism

Three steps from sample to signal.

A graphene field-effect transistor is, at heart, a charge sensor. We make it specific by anchoring receptor molecules to the channel. When a target binds, the device sees it — directly, electrically, without amplification.

STEP_01 t = 0s

Capture.

A functionalized graphene surface binds the target — bacterium, virus, fungal spore, or molecule.

STEP_02 t ≈ 02:00

Transduce.

The binding event shifts the electric field across the gFET channel — a measurable change in conductance.

§ 04 — The science

A transducer, made specific.

01 · Graphene as transducer

A single atomic layer of carbon places every electron at the surface, making the channel exquisitely sensitive to local charge. There is no bulk to dilute the signal. A single binding event can be detected as a measurable shift in conductance.

02 · Surface functionalization

Specificity is delivered by the receptor layer — antibodies, aptamers, or engineered binders covalently anchored to the graphene through a linker chemistry tuned for stability under food-matrix conditions. Swap the receptor; swap the target.

03 · Patented manufacturing

A patented low-cost process turns lab-grade gFETs into a producible, disposable test cartridge — the unit economics that lets the device live next to the production line, not on a research bench.

04 · Adaptable across analytes

The same platform answers different questions: bacteria today, viral particles tomorrow, mycotoxins the week after. Re-functionalize, re-validate, redeploy.
